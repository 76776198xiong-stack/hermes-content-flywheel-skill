# Publishing Rubric

Use this before finalizing content or when reviewing published performance.

## Topic

- Does the topic hit a real stuck point?
- Is there a specific audience?
- Is the creator's angle non-generic?
- Can the idea be explained in one sentence?
- Does it connect to the creator's larger business or worldview?

## Hook

Good hooks usually do one of these:

- name a familiar pain
- challenge a popular belief
- reveal a hidden variable
- point to a concrete scene
- state a sharp contradiction

Avoid:

- "今天我们来聊聊..."
- abstract thesis titles
- exaggerated promises
- hooks that attract the wrong audience

## Script

Check:

- one core insight only
- spoken rhythm
- concrete example or scene
- no unexplained jargon
- clear turn from common belief to deeper view
- useful ending, not empty motivation

## Title / Cover

Check:

- cover big text is short enough to read fast
- title creates curiosity without lying
- title and cover do not repeat the exact same sentence
- the promise matches the actual content

## CTA

CTA should match intent:

- trust content: soft CTA
- community answer: medium CTA
- launch or conversion content: direct CTA

Avoid making every piece sell. Repeated hard CTAs reduce trust.

## Review

When data is available:

| Signal | Possible meaning |
|---|---|
| high views, low completion | hook works, content or pacing weak |
| low views, high completion | topic/package weak, content may be good |
| high saves | practical value |
| high comments | conflict, identity, or unresolved question |
| high follows/leads | audience-product match |

Never conclude from one metric alone. Always include platform, topic type, packaging, and user's own feeling.
